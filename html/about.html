Origin story

I started coaching because I got tired of watching smart people fail at fitness.

Not because they lacked discipline. Not because they weren't trying hard enough. But because everything they were handed — the apps, the generic plans, the one-size-fits-all programmes — ignored the reality of their lives.

I saw it constantly. Friends working 12-hour days in finance downloading workout plans designed for people with three free hours a day. Colleagues trying to follow American meal prep videos with ingredients they couldn't find in Lagos. People who'd start strong, hit one disruption — travel, a deadline, a family crisis — and the whole thing would collapse because the plan had no flexibility built in.

I'd been there myself.

In my early twenties, I was working in corporate sales, putting in long hours, and watching my health slip. I tried the popular apps. I joined a gym I rarely used. I'd start programmes on Monday and abandon them by Thursday because they didn't account for the fact that some weeks I'd be traveling, some weeks I'd be slammed, and some weeks I'd just be too exhausted to think.

"Not more information. Not another app. A real person who understands their context and shows up consistently."

What finally worked for me wasn't finding the perfect programme. It was working with someone who built around my actual life. A coach who adjusted when things changed. Someone who kept me accountable when motivation disappeared.

That's when I realised: this is what most people need. Not more information. Not another app. A real person who understands their context and shows up consistently.

So I got certified. Started coaching part-time. Built a process that prioritised real life over theory. And six years later, this is all I do — because I've seen it work for over 150 professionals who were exactly where I used to be.

How I think

What I believe about fitness and coaching.

Fitness isn't complicated.
Life is.

Most people don't fail because they don't know what to do. They fail because the plan doesn't fit the reality of their week. My job isn't to give you the most cutting-edge programme or the most optimised protocol. It's to build something you can actually sustain — and then make sure you don't abandon it when life gets messy.

Generic
doesn't work.

You're not generic. Your schedule isn't generic. Your stress levels, your access to equipment, your food preferences, your history with fitness — none of it is generic. So why would I give you a template I've sent to 200 other people? Every programme I build is custom. Every check-in is personal. That's not a luxury. That's the baseline.

Accountability
is everything.

You already know you should work out. You already know you should eat better. Knowing isn't the problem. Doing it consistently when no one's watching — that's the problem. The reason weekly check-ins work is simple: you know someone's paying attention. You know I'll notice if you disappear. That small bit of external accountability is often the difference between quitting in week three and still going strong in month six.

I work best with people who
are ready to commit.

I don't need you to pretend you have unlimited time or motivation. I need you to tell me the truth about your schedule, your barriers, and what's actually hard for you. Then we build around that. If you're looking for someone to hold your hand and tell you it's okay to skip workouts, I'm not your coach. If you're looking for someone who'll build a plan for your reality — and hold you accountable to it — then we should talk.

Background

Six years. 150+ clients. One focus.

I'm a certified personal trainer and nutrition coach with six years of full-time coaching experience. I've worked with over 150 clients across West Africa — from Lagos to Accra to Abuja and beyond — helping busy professionals build sustainable fitness habits without upending their lives.

I've coached corporate executives who travel three weeks a month. New parents managing postpartum recovery with zero sleep. Entrepreneurs building startups on erratic schedules. Shift workers. Frequent flyers. People with home gyms, hotel gyms, and no gyms at all.

The common thread isn't the programme. It's the process: personalised planning, weekly accountability, and real-time adjustments when life happens. This isn't a side project. It's my full-time work.
